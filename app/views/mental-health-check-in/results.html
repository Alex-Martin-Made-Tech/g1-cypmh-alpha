Find support - results

Based on what you've told us, we think these are the best ways to get help with what you're feeling right now. You don't need anyone else to refer you to be able to use any of them.

Free online cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) course

CBT can help you manage your problems by changing the way you think and behave. NHS Partner getselfhelp.co.uk has a free, online course explaining CBT and helping you put it into practice.

Gloucestershire self-harm helpline

Phone, text and web-chat helpline. A trained mental health recovery worker will listen to you, support you, provide options and ways of coping and give information of other places that can help if needed. 5pm - 10pm every day.

tic+ - free counselling and helpline

tic+ provide counselling and an anonymous, safe, 1-2-1, online chat and telephone support service for young people with issues such as depression, anxiety, bereavement, bullying, anger, relationship problems, low self-esteem and self-harm.

Free online counselling and support network

Kooth provide free anonymous counselling for young people experiencing issues with their emotional wellbeing, such as anxiety, low mood or stress. There is no referral needed from a health professional, no waiting lists, and no criteria to meet to get support.

Don't think any of these sound right for you?

You can send your answers to the questions you've just answered directly to our text service, where you can discuss your options with a mental health expert.
